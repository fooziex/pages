About

All you could ever want to know about Rootnode

Rootnode The best shell account with Ruby, Python and PHP

Rootnode is a very unusual kind of shell hosting. Actually, we don't even like to call it hosting. We are social-oriented, we have subversive ideas and we are very eager to make this place unique.

We are social

We love to share knowledge and we like to work together. The most important part of Rootnode is its community. We obliterate the stereotype of lonely user sitting in front of a terminal. There are many other users you can communicate with in the old-fashioned way: using mailing lists and an IRC channel.

We do open source

A lot of people think that open source means for free. It doesn't. Rootnode is not "free as in 'free beer'" due to our many expenses. Rootnode has to pay for colocation, server leasing, electricity and bandwidth.

However, Rootnode is "free as in 'free speech'". Being social hosting, Rootnode makes everything we create available for free in public git repositories which can be found at https://github.com/rootnode.

Currently, we use the following git repositories:

  • lxc - linux containers related software, tools and configuration.
  • satan - shell account service manager.
  • scripts - small scripts and additional tools.
  • pages - this website and other Rootnode web pages.
Feel free to make improvements!

Our philosophy

Rootnode is a hobby project. We do it for fun. Never, ever consider Rootnode as a commercial or professional solution. We are untrustworthy, unreliable and you better have a backup :)

No clients

Although, we take money from you, you are not a client and we will never treat you like one. Please, don't behave like one. You are a user and community member. Be nice to us and we will be nice to you.

No guarantee

We do not guarantee anything - no SLA, no uptime, no anything. You will not get your money back when something fails. Consider yourself warned! This is not a joke. If you are demanding by nature, please, do not purchase an account.

We make mistakes and servers fail. If something does go wrong, you can always check our current status at http://rootnodestatus.net. We do not hide anything.

No official support

We are system administrators and, as such, we don't really have much time. Still, we read e-mails and we will probably answer yours but we cannot guarantee a time when it will happen.

However, there is some good news. We have active mailing lists which are a really good place to ask for help. Be kind, describe your problem precisely and remember to at least attach the following details:

  • uid and/or username
  • server name, e.g. web1
  • executed commands with full input and output
Important Do not hide or obscure any domain names; it will only make the problem harder to solve.

From scratch

We take all our experience and start again.

Rootnode originally started in the year 2006. From then to now, we have given our users a lot of freedom and access to a large number of services. Not only shell hosting but also application hosting, VPN, torrent, SHOUTcast and more.

Growing up in this uncontrolled way has also led us to multiple vulnerabilities and our systems were compromised eventually.

We decided to start over -- to rethink and do it from scratch. Needless to say, this process takes a lot of time and tremendous effort. Because of this, many services are currently unavailable, and have to be redesigned with security in mind.

Rootnode was entirely colocated in datacenter in Amsterdam, Netherlands. We have already invested a lot of money in our own hardware. Ultimately, we decided to power off this location and it is currently unavailable, waiting for a huge maintenance process and eventual reopening.Currently, we are building the new Rootnode on dedicated servers colocated in Europe.

Long story short:

  • We spin up our services one by one.
  • We use containers, a new virtualization technology to ensure user isolation.
  • We are working on fastweb platform to serve web traffic much faster.
  • We will kick ass again.
